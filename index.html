Executive Summary

Risk controls existed, but critical threats escalated before intervention.

15,000duplicate patient records required manual cleanup.
38%budget overrun after data, vendor, and integration issues compounded.
47critical workflow issues surfaced during end-user testing.

The HealthConnect EHR project used a risk register, monthly reviews, and escalation procedures, but several risks were underestimated or handled reactively. Data migration errors, integration failures, vendor resource shortfalls, and unclear communication delayed response and reduced clinical stakeholder visibility. Future implementations need earlier frontline involvement, enforceable vendor accountability, real-time dashboards, automated escalation thresholds, and balanced technical and stakeholder effectiveness metrics.

Monitoring Analysis

Monthly reports were not enough for fast-moving EHR risk.

What went wrong

  • Warning signs such as duplicate records, interface failures, and vendor capacity constraints were not tracked as real-time indicators.
  • Escalation pathways were inconsistently applied, leaving the team to solve high-impact issues internally for too long.
  • Technical updates did not translate risk into operational effects for clinicians and executives.

What to monitor next

  • Data migration error rates, manual cleanup effort, and legacy documentation gaps.
  • Vendor staffing commitments, sprint throughput, and custom report delivery progress.
  • Clinical readiness, medication workflow safety, uptime, interface stability, and audit readiness.

Recommendations

Move from predictive-only risk management to adaptive, visible controls.

1

Expand identification

Engage frontline users, physicians, IT, compliance, and vendors through interviews, brainstorming, scenario workshops, checklists, and lessons-learned reviews.

2

Audit early

Mandate data quality assessments, legacy walkthroughs, and proof-of-concept migration before execution.

3

Strengthen contracts

Require enforceable vendor resource guarantees, capacity reporting, and pre-approved backup support.

4

Automate escalation

Trigger executive intervention when timeline delays exceed 10%, budget variance exceeds 10%, or operational thresholds are breached.

5

Create CRIP coverage

Use an ad hoc response team with the Integration Lead, Security Manager, and IT Director for incidents such as duplicated medical records.
